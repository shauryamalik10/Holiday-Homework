Hydroponic Farming


What is Hydroponic Farming




Hydroponics is a way to skip the soil, sub in a different material to support the roots of the plant, and grow crops directly in nutrient-rich water. There are multiple approaches to designing hydroponic systems, but the core elements are essentially the same.

Benefits of Hydroponic Farming

  1. In hydroponic systems, roots don’t need to spread because water and nutrients are delivered right to them. As a result, hydroponic systems can grow more plants in the same amount of space as soil-based systems

  2. Hydroponic systems use about 10 times less water because it’s delivered in a controlled way. Plus, some systems recirculate water, reducing consumption even more!

  3. Hydroponic systems are highly controlled so there’s no risk of weeds taking over your garden. As a result, there’s no need for herbicides. Also, because hydroponic systems are often indoors, pests can’t infiltrate them easily, so pesticides aren’t necessary.

  4. Plants grown in hydroponic systems grow 30% - 50% times faster than those grown in soil.

  5. In hydroponic farming, plants grow healthier than in soil.

  6. Field agricultural practices have eroded half of the soil on Earth in the last 150 years, decreasing the availability of arable land. Hydroponic systems don’t use soil and that means no soil erosion.
